Covid-19 Safety Precautions

How Many Covid-19 Symptoms Can You Name?

A wide range of symptoms have been reported, ranging from mild symptoms to severe illnesses. Symptoms include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Abuse & Domestic Violence

If a current or former partner is hurting you or your children, or threatening to hurt you or your children, please reach out for help. There are many resources available to make sure that you and your children can be safe.

If you are looking for support or someone to talk to:

National Domestic Violence Hotline

Call 1.800.799.7233 and TTY 1.800.787.3224 (confidential and free). If you are unable to speak safely, you can log onto the hotline or text LOVEIS to22522.

Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline

Call 1.800.422.4453 anytime to speak to a counselor. Options for live chat and texting are available as well on the Childhelp website.

National Sexual Assault Hotline

Call 1.800.656.4673 anytime to receive free, confidential support from a counselor.

Rape Crisis Center

The Rape Crisis Center will be offering all their free and confidential counseling services via phone and/or telehealth using Zoom Video Conferencing. Call 210.349.7273 anytime to speak to an advocate.

Family Violence Prevention Services

Call 210.733.8810 anytime to speak to crisis intervention specialist.

StrongHearts Native Helpline for domestic/sexual violence

Confidential hotline specifically for native communities.Call 1.844.762.8483 between 7:00 a.m. – 10:00 p.m. daily to speak to an advocate.

Trans LifeLine

This hotline is staffed exclusively by transgender operators, and provides emotional support and financial resources to trans people. Call 1.877.565.8860 between 9:00 a.m. – 3:00 a.m. daily to speak to an advocate.

When to seek emergency medical attention

Look for emergency warning signs* for COVID-19. If someone is showing any of these signs, seek emergency medical care immediately:

  • Trouble breathing
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion
  • Inability to wake or stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face

*This list is not all possible symptoms. Please call your medical provider for any other symptoms that are severe or concerning to you.

Call 911 or call ahead to your local emergency facility: Notify the operator that you are seeking care for someone who has or may have COVID-19.

If you're unsure, visit the CDC symptoms page, your source for reliable Covid-19 information.
